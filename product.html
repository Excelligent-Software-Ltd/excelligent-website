The Science of Retention

Cadmus Spelling is built on decades of cognitive science research into how humans learn and remember.

Spaced Repetition

Rather than cramming information, we review material at increasing intervals. This spacing effect dramatically improves long-term retention by strengthening memory consolidation.

Active Recall

Students actively retrieve spellings from memory rather than passively reading them. This retrieval practice is one of the most effective learning strategies identified by research.

Immediate Feedback

Instant correction prevents the reinforcement of incorrect spellings and helps students identify and fix errors in real-time.

Adaptive Learning

The system responds to individual performance, adjusting difficulty and review frequency to optimise each student's learning curve.
